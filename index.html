Part One
Segmented Email
11 segments pulled directly from CampBrain data. Every email has a specific audience, a specific reason, and a specific call to action. Zero generic blasts. Sends go out Friday mornings.

The Core Philosophy

Relevance is everything. A parent whose child just turned 7 doesn't need the same email as a family who attended last week. CampBrain holds DOB, program history, location, attendance years, and registration dates. We use every field. Each segment below has the exact CampBrain filter so Macy can pull the list in minutes.

New SegmentUpsellSend: April + Post-WeekEst. 150–300 families

Single-week families aren't price-resistant — they just never had a reason to book more. A 10% saving on a second week reframes the decision from "do I want more camp?" to "why wouldn't I save the money?"
When to send
Once in April as a proactive offer. Then again as a post-week upsell — sent the Monday after their first week ends, while the kid is still buzzing about it. Two shots at the same segment.
CampBrain Filter

Every Friday morning during camp season — short, plain-text, casual. Not designed. One photo from that week's camp. Which programs have spots open for upcoming weeks. One registration link. Part Two
Getting Into Schools
Staff who are teachers, parents who are admins, and existing foundation relationships get BeachSports into 15+ schools without a single cold call to a principal.

The Strategic Reality

Schools are gatekept — principals are bombarded with flyer requests. The businesses that get in are the ones with a warm connection and zero friction for the school. BeachSports has both. Foundation donations = credibility. Staff/parent relationships = the door. The flyer just needs to make it easy to say yes.

1
Tier 1 · Easiest · Do First
Activate the Internal Network

Staff members who are teachers and parents who are school admins have direct access to school communication channels. Give each connection a ready-to-forward email template — you do the work, they just hit send. Pre-write the email for them including talking point: "BeachSports donates to our school foundation every year — they just want families to know about summer programs."

2
Tier 2 · Foundation Leverage · May
Formal Ask Through Foundation Partners

BeachSports donates to MBEF, RBEF, HBEF, Peninsula Ed Foundation, ES Ed Foundation, Hermosa Beach Ed Foundation, and American Martyrs Foundation. Email each Executive Director, reference the donation, ask for May bag stuffing or newsletter inclusion. Three foundation emails = 12+ schools covered.

The exact ask
Ask for one of three things: bag stuffing in May, inclusion in parent newsletter, or mention in end-of-year email blast. They will say yes — you're a donor asking a small favor.
Flyer specs
Design in Canva. Double-sided: programs + QR code front, summer checklist back. Print 500–1,000 at Costco Business (~$50–80). Deliver in person, pre-sorted by classroom.
3
Tier 3 · Direct Outreach · April–May
Office Manager Outreach (Not the Principal)

For schools not covered by foundations. The office manager — not the principal — controls bulletin boards, bag stuffing, and school newsletters. Brief friendly email + in-person delivery. Show up with a sorted stack and a BeachSports tee as a thank-you for the office. Schools that meet you in person distribute 3x more reliably.

03
Part Three
NextDoor + Facebook Communities
The channels where South Bay parents already talk to each other. Show up like a neighbor, not a brand. Combined potential reach: 15,000–30,000 local parents.

The Mindset Shift

NextDoor and Facebook community groups are trust-based environments. The businesses that win here show up as community members first, businesses second. BeachSports is perfectly positioned — you donate to foundations, you employ local kids, you're part of the South Bay fabric. Lead with that identity in every post.

1
NextDoor · Free · 15-Min Setup
NextDoor Business Profile

Go to nextdoor.com/business → 112 Arena St, El Segundo → Recreation/Camps → verify. Free forever. Post 1–2x per week during summer as a neighbor: "Spots open at Hermosa Surf this week 🤙" Never use promotional language. Encourage happy parents to post their own NextDoor recommendations — peer-to-peer posts reach 500+ households and don't get flagged.

2
Facebook Groups · Free · High Reach
Community Group Strategy

South Bay Moms, Manhattan Beach Parents, Redondo Beach Families, Hermosa Beach Community, El Segundo Parents — combined reach 15,000–30,000. Groups ban direct ads but allow community recommendations. Have Macy and coaches join personally and post authentically. Answer every "summer camp recommendations?" thread immediately. These threads appear constantly in May and June.

3
Facebook Marketplace · Free · 10-Min Setup
Marketplace Services Listing

Marketplace → Services → Recreation. 3–5 camp photos, description, price, registration link. Relist every 10 days to stay at top of search results. Almost no local camps are using Marketplace — it's an untapped channel that parents actively search when looking for local kids' activities.

Both ads are designed to sell out all programs equally — but Volleyball gets its own dedicated spotlight within the Instagram campaign because it's the lowest-registration program and needs the most awareness lift. Google Search captures parents already searching by program name, so all five programs get their own keywords. Every ad dollar is working for the whole lineup.
