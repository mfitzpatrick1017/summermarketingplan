Tactical · July 4th Week · Single Day
July 4th Week — Single Day Camp Push
Send: Late JuneFull listNew Strategy
▼
Strategy
The week of July 4th is historically low enrollment — a single-day offering removes the "I'm not sure about a full week" barrier and fills slots that would otherwise sit empty.
How
Send to full list late June — promote a single-day camp option for that week. Low commitment for the family, still fills revenue. Keep subject line holiday-fun, not salesy.
Why single day works here: Families have July 4th weekend plans but may have open days mid-week. A "drop in for a day" option is an easy yes — especially for parents who want their kid active while they work. This also introduces new families to BeachSports who then register for a full week later.
Subject Line
"No plans July [day]? We've got a day at the beach 🎆"
The week of July 4th we're running single-day camp sessions — a perfect way to keep the summer going without committing to a full week...

02
Part Two
Getting Into Schools
15+ schools across all three cities. No cold calls to principals needed — foundation relationships and internal connections open every door.
1
Tier 1 · Easiest · Do First
Activate Internal Network
  • Who: Staff members who are teachers + parents who are school admins
  • The ask: Forward a pre-written email from Macy to their principal or office manager — 30 seconds for them
  • Give them: Pre-written email template + digital PDF flyer + physical flyer stack
  • Talking point: "BeachSports donates to our school foundation every year — they want families to know about summer programs"
2
Tier 2 · Foundation Leverage · May
Email Foundation Partners Directly
  • Contact: MBEF, RBEF, HBEF, Peninsula Ed Foundation, ES Ed Foundation, AMS Foundation
  • Ask for one of: May bag stuffing / parent newsletter mention / end-of-year email blast inclusion
  • Result: 3 foundation emails = 12+ schools covered automatically — they will say yes, you're a donor
  • Flyer: Design in Canva → double-sided (programs + QR code front, summer checklist back)
  • Print: 500–1,000 at Costco Business Center (~$50–80) → deliver in person, pre-sorted by classroom, by May 9
3
Tier 3 · Direct · April–May
Office Manager Outreach (Not the Principal)
  • Contact: Office manager — they control bag stuffing, bulletin boards, and parent newsletters
  • Email: "BeachSports donates to [School] Foundation — happy to drop off flyers pre-sorted by classroom"
  • Delivery: Show up in person with sorted stack + BeachSports tee for the office — 3x more reliable than mailing
  • Digital: Ask about lobby display boards — a 10-second branded slide with QR code runs on loop in front of every drop-off parent

Part Three
NextDoor + Facebook Communities
Combined reach: 15,000–30,000 South Bay parents. All free. Show up like a neighbor, not a brand — that's the only way this works.
1
NextDoor · Free · 15 Min Setup
NextDoor Business Profile
  • Setup: nextdoor.com/business → 112 Arena St, El Segundo → Recreation/Camps → verify by phone. Free forever.
  • Post: 1–2x per week during summer — neighbor tone only. "Spots open at Hermosa Surf 🤙" + real photo
  • Never post: "REGISTER NOW — LIMITED SPOTS!!" — gets flagged as spam
  • Best move: Ask happy parents to post their own NextDoor recs — peer posts reach 500+ households and don't get flagged
2
Facebook Groups · Free · High Reach
Community Groups Strategy
  • Groups: South Bay Moms · Manhattan Beach Parents · Redondo Beach Families · Hermosa Beach Community · El Segundo Parents
  • Rule: Groups ban direct ads — Macy and coaches join personally and post authentically as members
  • Do: Answer every "summer camp recommendations?" thread immediately — these appear constantly in May and June
  • Share: Kid of the Week posts after publishing on Instagram
3
Facebook Marketplace · Free · 10 Min Setup
Services Listing
  • Setup: BeachSports FB page → Marketplace → Services → Recreation → 3–5 camp photos + description + price + reg link
  • Maintain: Relist every 10 days — 30 seconds, keeps BeachSports at top of search results
  • Advantage: Almost no local camps use Marketplace — untapped channel with parents actively searching

Two campaigns. All 5 programs covered. Volleyball gets dedicated extra budget — lowest enrollment, needs most awareness. Goal: brand awareness + new family acquisition.

The Approach

Google Search captures parents already searching by program name — highest intent possible. Instagram Feed reaches new South Bay families who haven't found BeachSports yet. $250–350/month total. Tight geo-targeting. Measurable from day one.

Registered Families · Separate Track
Post-Registration Email Flow
6 automated emails triggered after registration. Set up once in Active Campaign — runs all summer for every family automatically. Builds trust, reduces first-day anxiety, and drives merch + upsell revenue before camp starts.

How the Automation Works

These sends are completely separate from the marketing outreach track. Once a family registers, they enter an Active Campaign sequence. Emails trigger by days since registration or days before their camp week — never manual. Set up in April, runs automatically for every family all summer long.
