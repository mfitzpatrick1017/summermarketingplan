The Big Picture
Summer 2026 Game Plan
Registration is open. The job now is filling every spot, every week across Redondo, Hermosa, and Manhattan Beach. Four integrated parts — each one feeds the next.
Where We Are Right Now

Registration is live and the early-bird window has closed. The opportunity: convert warm leads who've been considering it, capture new South Bay families who don't know BeachSports yet, and make sure no open spot sits unfilled for more than 72 hours.

11 distinct segments from CampBrain. Every email has a specific audience, a specific reason, and a specific CTA. Zero generic blasts. Every send should feel like it was written personally for that family — because it was.

The Core Philosophy

The difference between a good email program and a great one is relevance. CampBrain holds DOB, program history, location history, and attendance years. We use every field. A parent whose kid just turned 7 doesn't need the same email as a cold lead who's never registered. Pull the right list, write the right email, send at the right moment.

Part Two
Getting Into Schools
Staff members who are teachers, parents who are school admins, and existing foundation relationships get BeachSports into 15+ schools without a single cold call to a principal.

The Strategic Reality

Schools are gatekept — principals and office managers are bombarded with flyer requests. The businesses that get in are the ones with a warm connection, a community angle, and zero friction for the school. BeachSports has all three. Foundation donations = credibility. Staff/parent relationships = the door. The flyer just needs to make it easy to say yes.

1
Tier 1 · Easiest · Do First
Activate the Internal Network

BeachSports has staff members who are teachers and parents who are school admins. These people have direct access to school communication channels — weekly newsletters, parent email blasts, hallway bulletin boards, and bag stuffing. This is the fastest path into schools and requires zero cold outreach.

The ask (make it a 30-second favor)
Give each connection a ready-to-send email template they can forward to their principal or office manager. You do the work — they just hit send. Pre-write the email for them.
What to give them
A pre-written email. A digital flyer (PDF). A stack of physical flyers. One talking point: "BeachSports donates to our school foundation every year — they just want families to know about summer programs."
2
Tier 2 · Foundation Leverage · May
Formal Ask Through Foundation Partners

BeachSports already donates to MBEF, RBEF, HBEF, Peninsula Ed Foundation, ES Ed Foundation, Hermosa Beach Ed Foundation, and American Martyrs School Foundation. Each foundation has direct communication channels to every parent at every school they serve. One ask per foundation = 10–15 schools covered.

The exact ask
Email the Executive Director of each foundation. Reference the donation relationship. Ask for one of: bag stuffing in May, inclusion in parent newsletter, or a mention in end-of-year email blast. They will say yes — you're a donor asking a small favor.
Schools this unlocks
MBEF → all MB elementaries + Mira Costa. RBEF → Redondo elementaries. HBEF → Hermosa Valley + View. Three emails to three foundation directors = 12+ schools.

Tier 3 · Direct Outreach · April–May
Office Manager Outreach (Not the Principal)

For schools not covered by foundations or internal connections, go direct — but to the right person: the office manager, not the principal. They control bulletin boards, bag stuffing, and parent newsletters. A brief, friendly email with a community angle and donation mention works far better than a generic cold flyer drop.

The email script
"Hi [Name], BeachSports is a family-owned South Bay camp — we support [School] Foundation every year. We'd love to include a flyer in May bags. Happy to drop them off pre-sorted by classroom." Pre-sorting removes all effort from them.
Physical delivery strategy
Don't mail — deliver in person. Show up with a sorted stack and a BeachSports tee as a thank-you for the office. Schools that meet you in person distribute 3x more reliably than those who just receive a mailed package.
4
Tier 4 · Digital · Long Game
School Newsletters + Digital Lobby Boards

Many South Bay schools send weekly parent email newsletters and have digital display boards in their lobbies. A single paragraph in a school newsletter reaches 400–800 families at once, for free. A 10-second branded slide on a lobby display board runs on loop in front of every parent at drop-off and pickup.

How to get in the newsletter
Ask foundation contacts to connect you to the school newsletter editor. Frame as a community resource announcement: "BeachSports, a South Bay-founded camp and foundation supporter, has spots available for summer..."
Digital lobby boards
Ask if community businesses can submit a slide (many schools allow this). A branded 10-second slide with a photo of kids surfing + QR code runs on loop. Free billboard at every South Bay school that allows it.

Part Three
NextDoor, Facebook Communities
The channels where South Bay parents already talk to each other. Getting here isn't about ads — it's about showing up like a neighbor, not a brand. Combined potential reach: 15,000–30,000 local parents.

The Mindset Shift

NextDoor and Facebook community groups are trust-based environments. People are there for neighbor-to-neighbor conversation — they instantly distrust anything that feels like advertising. The businesses that win here are the ones that show up as a community member first, business second. BeachSports is perfectly positioned to do this authentically.

1
NextDoor · Free Business Profile
NextDoor for Business — Setup + Strategy

NextDoor has a free Business profile that lets local businesses post to specific neighborhoods. BeachSports' El Segundo address qualifies. Set up one profile, target posts to Redondo Beach, Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, and El Segundo neighborhoods. Requirements are minimal — 15 minutes to set up, free forever.

Exact setup steps
Go to nextdoor.com/business → Business name (BeachSports) → Address (112 Arena St, El Segundo) → Phone → Category: Recreation / Summer Camps → Verify via postcard or phone call. Done. Free.
What to post (and what not to)
Post like a neighbor, not a brand. "Spots open at Hermosa Surf this week if anyone's looking 🤙" works. "REGISTER NOW — LIMITED SPOTS!!" gets flagged as spam. Authentic + local always wins.
Posting frequency
1–2x per week during summer. More feels spammy. One real photo of a local kid outperforms five generic posts.
The secret weapon
Encourage happy parents to post their own NextDoor recommendations. A neighbor recommending BeachSports reaches 500+ households and NextDoor won't flag it as advertising. Worth asking for directly.
2
Facebook Groups · Free · High Reach
Community Groups — The Right Strategy

South Bay has several high-membership parent Facebook groups with combined reach of 15,000–30,000 local parents. Most groups ban direct business advertising — but allow community members to share resources and recommendations. The strategy is staff and parent advocates posting organically, not BeachSports posting as a brand.

Key groups to target
South Bay Moms · Manhattan Beach Parents · Redondo Beach Families · Hermosa Beach Community · South Bay Kids Activities · El Segundo Parents. Have Macy and coaches join personally and post authentically.
What works in groups
"My daughter just finished her first week of BeachSports surf camp — she's obsessed" posted by a real parent is not an ad, it's a recommendation. Answer every "summer camp recommendations?" thread immediately. They appear constantly in May/June.

The Golden Rule · All Community Channels
Give Before You Ask

The brands that win on community platforms give value before they ask for anything. BeachSports already does this — you donate to foundations, you employ local kids, you're woven into the South Bay community fabric. Lean into that identity in every post. Be useful and local first. The registration link comes second.

Content that earns trust
Ocean safety tips. Shoutouts to local lifeguards. Staff highlights. Kid of the Week. "Proud of our coaches this summer." Community-first content builds the credibility that turns a scroll into a registration.
Content that gets removed
"Sign up now! Limited spots!" posted directly in groups gets flagged as spam. Never lead with a sales message in community spaces. Lead with the story, end with the link — always.

Part Four
Paid Ads — 2 Max
Goal: brand awareness — getting BeachSports in front of South Bay families who don't know you yet. Two ads, done well, beat five ads done poorly. Google Search + Instagram Feed.

The Paid Ads Philosophy

With a modest budget and a brand awareness goal, every dollar needs to reach a new local family — not retarget people who already know BeachSports. That means tight geographic targeting, strong creative, and platforms where South Bay parents actually spend time. Two ads max — Google Search for intent capture, Instagram Feed for awareness. Do both well rather than spreading thin.

Double down on the winner. If Google Search delivers one additional registration per week ($350+), it pays for itself entirely.
